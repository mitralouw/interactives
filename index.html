Americans Question Whether College is Worth the Cost

By Mitra Nourbakhsh

Over the last ten years, economic outcomes for Americans without college degrees are improving. Some young adults are beginning to question the importance of pursuing further education, according to a recent Pew Research study.

Only one-in-four U.S. adults say it’s extremely or very important to have a four-year college degree in order to get a well-paying job in today’s economy. Data shows that earnings of young men without a college degree have, in fact, been on the rise.

    • It’s extremely or very likely someone without a four-year college degree can get a well-paying job (42% vs. 26%)
